SUSTAINABLE FARMING PRACTICES — INDIA
Source: ICAR, National Institute of Organic Farming (NIOF), FAO India

=== INTEGRATED FARMING SYSTEMS ===

What is IFS?
Integrated Farming System combines crops, animals, fish, poultry, and trees on the same farm to:
- Maximize productivity per unit area
- Reduce external inputs
- Improve income stability
- Sustain natural resources

Common IFS models in India:
1. Crop + Dairy: Crops provide fodder; cow dung provides manure
2. Crop + Poultry: Poultry manure enriches soil; grain for feed
3. Rice + Fish: Fish in paddy fields; fish feed on insects/weeds
4. Agroforestry: Trees on field bunds (Moringa, Mango, Drumstick)

=== SOIL HEALTH MANAGEMENT ===

Principles for healthy soil:
1. Minimize tillage — preserves soil structure and biology
2. Keep soil covered — prevent erosion; use mulch or cover crops
3. Maintain crop diversity — rotation prevents pest buildup
4. Keep living roots in soil year-round
5. Integrate animals — manure adds organic matter

Measuring soil health:
- Soil Organic Carbon (SOC): Target > 0.75%
- Soil pH: Ideal 6.0–7.5 for most crops
- Soil EC: < 0.8 dS/m (non-saline)
- Earthworm count: > 10/sq foot = healthy soil

Improving soil health:
- Green manuring: Dhaincha, Sunhemp before kharif
- Composting: Kitchen + farm waste → nutrient-rich compost (45–60 days)
- Biofertilizers: Trichoderma, Rhizobium, PSB, Azatobacter
- Avoid burning crop residue — incorporate into soil

=== CROP ROTATION BENEFITS ===

Why rotate crops?
- Breaks pest and disease cycles (e.g., nematodes, soil-borne fungi)
- Improves soil fertility (legumes fix nitrogen)
- Reduces weed pressure
- Maintains soil structure

Recommended rotations:
- Rice → Wheat → Mung (NW India)
- Cotton → Chickpea (Maharashtra/MP)
- Maize → Mustard → Mung
- Sugarcane → Wheat (UP, Bihar)
- Rice → Vegetables → Groundnut (South India)

Do NOT plant the same family consecutively:
- Solanaceae: Tomato, Potato, Brinjal, Pepper
- Cucurbitaceae: Cucumber, Gourd, Watermelon

=== CLIMATE-SMART AGRICULTURE ===

Adapting to Climate Change:
1. Drought-tolerant varieties: Sahbhagi Dhan, MACS 6222, K307 (wheat)
2. Flood-tolerant varieties: Swarna Sub-1, Jalmagna rice
3. Short-duration varieties: Complete before predicted drought/flood
4. Zero Tillage (wheat after rice): Saves 1500–2000 L water/ha; reduces GHG

Reducing Greenhouse Gas Emissions:
- AWD (Alternate Wetting Drying) for rice: 25–30% less methane
- Direct Seeded Rice (DSR): Less water; less GHG
- Crop residue incorporation vs burning

=== POST-HARVEST MANAGEMENT ===

Why post-harvest management matters:
- India loses 10–30% of fruits and vegetables post-harvest
- Total post-harvest losses: ~₹1.5 lakh crore/year

Key practices:
1. Proper harvesting at right maturity
2. Temperature management: Pre-cooling; cold chain
3. Grading and sorting: Uniform size/quality = better price
4. Packaging: Ventilated crates reduce respiration losses
5. Storage: Warehousing; Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA

Storage options:
- Traditional: Kothi (bamboo bin), Ghara (clay pot), Bukhari
- Modern: CWC/SWC warehouses; controlled atmosphere storage
- Cold storage: Potato, Onion, Fruits; available through NABARD subsidy

=== MARKET LINKAGE ===

Getting best price for produce:
1. Form Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs): Collective bargaining power
2. Use e-NAM (National Agriculture Market): Pan-India transparent bidding
3. Contract Farming: Price assured before sowing
4. Value Addition: Sell processed products (flour, jam, pickle) at higher margins
5. Self Help Groups (SHGs): Collective selling; reduced transport costs

APMC (Agricultural Produce Market Committee):
- Mandated marketing yards in each district
- Farmers must receive MSP (Minimum Support Price) or above
- MSP 2024–25 examples:
  - Wheat: ₹2,275/quintal
  - Rice: ₹2,300/quintal
  - Soybean: ₹4,892/quintal
  - Cotton: ₹7,121/quintal (F-1054 variety)

=== PRECISION AGRICULTURE ===

Technologies for small farmers:
1. Soil sensors / EC meters: Measure nutrient status in field
2. Weather stations: Farm-level microclimate data
3. Drone spraying: Reduces pesticide use by 25–30%
4. GPS-guided tractors: Precision land preparation
5. Remote sensing: Satellite-based crop health monitoring (FASAL, Bhuvan)

Mobile apps for farmers:
- mKisan: Government advisories
- Kisan Suvidha: Weather, inputs, market prices
- e-Kheti: Crop advisory
- FASAL: Crop forecasting
- AgriApp: Disease diagnosis

=== ORGANIC CERTIFICATION ===

Process for organic certification in India:
1. PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System): Local certification; 1–3 years
2. NPOP (National Programme for Organic Production): For export; 3 years conversion
3. Third-party certification bodies: APOF, IMO, OneCert, ECOCERT

Steps:
1. Register with nearest certification body or KVK
2. Maintain field diary / farm records for 3 years
3. No prohibited substances for conversion period
4. Annual inspection and soil/product testing

Premium for organic produce: 20–40% higher than conventional
