IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT GUIDE — INDIA
Source: ICAR, Central Water Commission, INCID (Indian National Committee on Irrigation and Drainage)

=== IMPORTANCE OF IRRIGATION ===

India has 140 million hectares of net sown area, but only 70 million ha is under assured irrigation.
Proper irrigation management can:
- Increase crop yields by 40–60%
- Save 20–40% water through efficient methods
- Reduce water-related crop losses

=== IRRIGATION METHODS ===

1. FLOOD / SURFACE IRRIGATION
- Traditional method; suitable for rice, sugarcane
- Water Use Efficiency (WUE): 35–40%
- Cost: Low
- Suitable soils: Clay, clay loam (low permeability)

2. DRIP IRRIGATION
- Water delivered directly to root zone
- WUE: 85–90%
- Saves 40–50% water vs flood irrigation
- Yield increase: 20–40%
- Best for: Cotton, vegetables, fruits, sugarcane, banana
- Subsidy: 40–90% under PMKSY

3. SPRINKLER IRRIGATION
- Water sprayed as artificial rain
- WUE: 70–75%
- Saves 30–40% water
- Best for: Wheat, groundnut, soybean, vegetables
- Not suitable for: Rice, jute (needs flooding)
- Subsidy available under PMKSY

4. ALTERNATE WETTING AND DRYING (AWD) — Rice
- Let water level drop to 15 cm below surface, then re-flood
- Saves 15–30% water
- No yield loss if done correctly
- Reduces methane emissions

=== CROP-WISE IRRIGATION SCHEDULES ===

RICE
Total water: 1200–2000 mm
- Puddling: Saturate soil 5–7 days before transplanting
- Vegetative stage: 5 cm standing water
- AWD method: Let drop to -15 cm, then irrigate
- Flowering (7 days): Ensure continuous shallow flooding
- Grain filling: Maintain 2–3 cm water
- Stop irrigation 10–12 days before harvest
Critical stages: Transplanting, tillering, panicle initiation, flowering

WHEAT
Total water: 400–500 mm (4–6 irrigations)
1st irrigation: Crown Root Initiation (20–25 DAS) — MOST CRITICAL
2nd irrigation: Tillering (40–45 DAS)
3rd irrigation: Jointing (60–65 DAS)
4th irrigation: Flowering (80–85 DAS) — critical
5th irrigation: Grain filling (100–105 DAS)
6th irrigation: Dough stage (115–120 DAS)
Note: 1 missed irrigation at CRI stage can reduce yield by 20%

COTTON
Total water: 700–1200 mm
- Pre-sowing: Thorough soaking
- Vegetative (4 weeks): Every 10–12 days
- Boll development: Every 8–10 days (critical)
- Boll opening: Reduce frequency
- Stop 3 weeks before harvest
Drip recommended: 4–6 L/plant/day

SUGARCANE
Total water: 1500–2500 mm
- Germination: 4–5 irrigations (every 7 days)
- Grand growth: Every 10–12 days
- Maturity: Reduce; stop 45 days before harvest
Furrow/drip preferred

MAIZE
Total water: 500–800 mm
- Germination: Keep moist; avoid waterlogging
- Knee-high stage: Every 7–8 days
- Tasseling/silking: Every 5 days (MOST CRITICAL — water stress = 40% yield loss)
- Grain filling: Every 8–10 days
- Dough stage: Reduce frequency

TOMATO
Total water: 400–600 mm
- Seedling establishment: Daily light irrigation
- Vegetative: Every 4–5 days
- Flowering: Every 3 days (critical)
- Fruit set/development: Every 4 days
- Avoid water stress or excess during flowering (causes blossom drop)
- Drip + fertigation strongly recommended

GROUNDNUT
Total water: 500–700 mm
1st irrigation: 7–10 DAS (germination)
2nd: Branching stage (25–30 DAS)
3rd: Flowering (45–50 DAS) — critical
4th: Pegging/pod development (70–75 DAS) — critical
5th: Pod filling (90–95 DAS)
Avoid waterlogging during pegging stage

=== WATER STRESS IDENTIFICATION ===
Visual signs that crop needs water:
- Leaf rolling or wilting during day (Turgor pressure loss)
- Leaf color turns blue-green or dull
- Afternoon wilting that doesn't recover by evening
- Dry soil at 10–15 cm depth (finger/soil probe test)

=== WATER CONSERVATION TECHNIQUES ===

1. Mulching
- Organic: Straw, paddy husk, sugarcane trash (3–5 cm layer)
- Plastic: Black or silver mulch film
- Benefit: Reduces evaporation by 30–40%; suppresses weeds

2. Laser Land Leveling
- Reduces water use by 20–25%
- Ensures uniform distribution
- Cost: ₹2,000–3,000/ha (subsidized)

3. Rainwater Harvesting
- Farm ponds: Store runoff water
- Cost: ₹100–200/m³ (subsidized under MGNREGS)

4. Soil Moisture Conservation
- Deep plowing breaks hard pan
- Contour bunding in hilly areas
- Broad bed and furrow (BBF) system

5. Tensiometer Use
- Measure soil moisture at root zone
- Irrigate at 0.4–0.6 bar (varies by crop)
- Avoids both under and over-irrigation

=== IRRIGATION WATER QUALITY ===
- EC < 1.2 dS/m: Suitable for all crops
- EC 1.2–3.0 dS/m: Moderate; use salt-tolerant crops
- EC > 3.0 dS/m: Problematic; needs treatment or blending
- pH 6.5–8.5: Acceptable range
- Check for heavy metals if using industrial effluent
