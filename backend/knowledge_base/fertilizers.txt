FERTILIZER AND SOIL NUTRITION GUIDE — INDIA
Source: ICAR, Soil Science Society of India, State Agriculture Universities

=== MACRO NUTRIENTS ===

NITROGEN (N)
Function: Vegetative growth, leaf greenness (chlorophyll), protein synthesis
Deficiency symptoms: General yellowing (chlorosis) starting from lower/older leaves; stunted growth
Common sources:
- Urea: 46% N (most popular, cheapest)
- DAP: 18% N + 46% P2O5
- CAN (Calcium Ammonium Nitrate): 26% N
- Ammonium Sulfate: 21% N
Organic: FYM (10–15 ton/ha), compost, poultry manure (2.5% N), vermicompost
Application tips:
- Split application reduces losses (base + 1–2 top dressings)
- Apply urea in evening; irrigate after application
- Avoid broadcast in waterlogged fields (denitrification)

PHOSPHORUS (P)
Function: Root development, energy transfer, flowering, seed formation
Deficiency symptoms: Purple/reddish tinge on leaf undersides; poor rooting; delayed maturity
Sources:
- DAP (Di-Ammonium Phosphate): 18-46-0
- SSP (Single Super Phosphate): 16% P2O5 + 11% S (provides sulfur too)
- TSP (Triple Super Phosphate): 46% P2O5
Organic: Rock phosphate, bone meal, PSB biofertilizer
Application tips:
- Apply at basal (at sowing/transplanting)
- PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria) biofertilizer helps in fixing soil phosphorus

POTASSIUM (K)
Function: Water regulation, enzyme activation, fruit quality, disease resistance
Deficiency symptoms: Brown scorching of leaf margins/tips; weak stems; poor quality produce
Sources:
- MOP (Muriate of Potash): 60% K2O
- SOP (Sulfate of Potash): 50% K2O (preferred for fruits/vegetables)
Organic: Wood ash, compost, banana stem

SECONDARY NUTRIENTS

Sulfur (S)
- Deficiency: Uniform yellowing of young leaves
- Source: Gypsum (18% S), SSP, Ammonium Sulfate, Elemental Sulfur
- Dose: 20–40 kg S/ha (basal)
- Especially important for oilseeds (mustard, groundnut, soybean)

Calcium (Ca)
- Deficiency: Blossom end rot in tomato/capsicum; tip burn in cabbage
- Source: Lime, Gypsum, Calcium Nitrate
- Foliar: Calcium Nitrate 1% spray at fruiting

Magnesium (Mg)
- Deficiency: Interveinal chlorosis in older leaves
- Source: Magnesium Sulfate (Epsom Salt)
- Foliar: MgSO4 0.5% spray × 2–3 times

=== MICRO NUTRIENTS ===

ZINC (Zn) — Most common deficiency in India
- Deficiency: Brown rust-colored spots (Khaira in rice); stunted growth; small leaves
- Source: Zinc Sulfate (21% or 33%), Zinc chelate
- Soil: 25–50 kg ZnSO4/ha
- Foliar: 0.5% ZnSO4 spray × 3 times

IRON (Fe)
- Deficiency: Interveinal chlorosis starting from young leaves (yellow-green striping)
- Common in calcareous/alkaline soils
- Source: Ferrous Sulfate 19%; Fe-EDTA chelate
- Foliar: 0.5% FeSO4 spray × 3 times

BORON (B)
- Deficiency: "Heart rot" in beet/turnip; hollow stem in mustard; poor fruit set
- Source: Borax 10.5% B; Boric acid
- Dose: 1–1.5 kg B/ha soil; 0.1–0.2% foliar spray

MANGANESE (Mn)
- Deficiency: Interveinal chlorosis in young leaves; grayish specks
- Source: Manganese Sulfate
- Foliar: 0.3% spray

=== CROP-WISE NPK RECOMMENDATIONS (kg/ha) ===

Rice:         N:120, P:60, K:60
Wheat:        N:120, P:60, K:40
Maize:        N:150, P:75, K:75
Cotton:       N:160, P:80, K:80
Soybean:      N:30, P:60, K:60 (N from Rhizobium)
Groundnut:    N:25, P:50, K:50 + Gypsum 400 kg/ha
Sugarcane:    N:250, P:125, K:125
Tomato:       N:180, P:100, K:100
Potato:       N:180, P:80, K:100
Onion:        N:100, P:60, K:60
Mustard:      N:80, P:40, K:20

=== ORGANIC FARMING ===

Farmyard Manure (FYM)
- NPK: 0.5% N, 0.25% P, 0.5% K (approx)
- Application: 10–15 ton/ha; incorporate 3–4 weeks before sowing
- Benefits: Improves soil structure, water holding, microbial activity

Vermicompost
- NPK: 2–3% N, 1.5–2.5% P, 1.5–2% K
- Application: 5–10 ton/ha
- Rich in growth hormones and micronutrients

Green Manure
- Dhaincha (Sesbania): Incorporate at 45 days; adds 90–100 kg N/ha
- Sunhemp (Crotalaria): Adds 60–90 kg N/ha
- Berseem: Adds 30–50 kg N/ha

Biofertilizers
- Rhizobium: N-fixation for legumes; seed treatment 200 g/ha
- Azatobacter: Non-symbiotic N-fixation; 5 kg/ha; saves 20–25 kg N/ha
- PSB: Phosphate solubilization; 5 kg/ha; saves 20–25 kg P/ha
- VAM (Vesicular Arbuscular Mycorrhizae): Improves P, Zn uptake

Neem-based Products
- Neem Cake: 200–250 kg/ha; nitrification inhibitor; also pest deterrent
- Neem-coated Urea (NCU): Reduces N losses by 10–15%

=== SOIL HEALTH CARD SCHEME ===
All farmers should get their soil tested every 3 years.
Apply online at soilhealth.dac.gov.in
Benefits:
- Crop-wise fertilizer recommendations
- Soil pH, EC, and micronutrient status
- Saves 15–25% fertilizer cost
