PEST AND DISEASE MANAGEMENT GUIDE — INDIA
Source: ICAR, NCIPM (National Centre for Integrated Pest Management)

=== MAJOR CROP PESTS ===

RICE PESTS

1. Brown Plant Hopper (BPH)
   Cause: Nilaparvata lugens
   Symptoms: Circular yellowing patches ("hopperburn"), plants collapse
   Control:
   - Drain water from fields periodically
   - Spray Imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 0.3 ml/L or Buprofezin 25 SC @ 1 ml/L
   - Release egg parasitoid Anagrus spp.
   - Avoid excessive nitrogen
   Threshold: 5–10 hoppers/hill

2. Stem Borer
   Cause: Scirpophaga incertulas (yellow), Chilo suppressalis (striped)
   Symptoms: Dead hearts at vegetative; white ears at reproductive stage
   Control:
   - Release Trichogramma japonicum 50,000/ha weekly × 5
   - Chlorpyrifos 20 EC @ 2.5 ml/L
   - Cartap hydrochloride granules 4G @ 8 kg/ha
   Organic: NSKE (Neem Seed Kernel Extract) 5%

3. Leaf Folder
   Cause: Cnaphalocrocis medinalis
   Symptoms: Leaves rolled lengthwise; feeding damage inside fold
   Control:
   - Spray Chlorpyrifos 20 EC @ 2.5 ml/L
   - Quinalphos 25 EC @ 2 ml/L
   - Spray when 5–10% leaf damage

4. Blast (Leaf + Neck)
   Cause: Magnaporthe oryzae (fungus)
   Symptoms: Spindle-shaped gray-white spots; neck rot at grain filling
   Control:
   - Spray Tricyclazole 75 WP @ 0.6 g/L (3 sprays)
   - Isoprothiolane 40 EC @ 1.5 ml/L
   - Use resistant varieties: MTU-7029, Pusa Basmati 1509

WHEAT PESTS AND DISEASES

1. Yellow/Stripe Rust
   Cause: Puccinia striiformis (fungus)
   Symptoms: Yellow pustules in stripes on leaves
   Control:
   - Propiconazole 25 EC @ 0.1% (2 sprays, 10 days apart)
   - Tebuconazole 25.9 EC @ 0.1%
   - Use resistant varieties: PBW 343, HD 2967

2. Wheat Aphid
   Cause: Sitobion avenae
   Symptoms: Honeydew secretion; sooty mold; stunted ears
   Control:
   - Dimethoate 30 EC @ 1.5 ml/L
   - Spray when 50+ aphids/ear
   Organic: Neem oil 5 ml/L + soap solution

3. Loose Smut
   Cause: Ustilago segetum (seed-borne)
   Symptoms: Entire ear replaced by black powdery mass
   Control: Seed treatment — Carboxin + Thiram 37.5 + 37.5 DP @ 2 g/kg

COTTON PESTS

1. American Bollworm
   Cause: Helicoverpa armigera
   Symptoms: Bored squares, flowers, bolls; frass visible
   Control:
   - Indoxacarb 14.5 SC @ 0.7 ml/L
   - Emamectin benzoate 5 SG @ 0.4 g/L
   - Pheromone traps @ 5/ha for monitoring
   IPM: Ha-NPV (nuclear polyhedrosis virus) 250 LE/ha

2. Mealy Bug
   Cause: Phenacoccus solenopsis
   Symptoms: White cottony mass on plants; severe stunting
   Control:
   - Imidacloprid 70 WS seed treatment
   - Dimethoate 30 EC @ 2 ml/L
   - Augment Cryptolaemus montrouzieri (predatory beetle)

VEGETABLE PESTS

1. Diamondback Moth (DBM) — Crucifer vegetables
   Cause: Plutella xylostella
   Symptoms: Window-pane effect; peppering on leaves
   Control:
   - Spinosad 45 SC @ 0.3 ml/L
   - Flubendiamide 39.35 SC @ 0.2 ml/L
   Resistance management: Rotate insecticides with different modes of action

2. Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus (TYLCV)
   Cause: Begomovirus (whitefly-transmitted)
   Symptoms: Yellow curling leaves; stunting; no fruits set
   Control:
   - Control vector with Imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 0.5 ml/L
   - Use virus-free certified seedlings
   - Reflective mulch to repel whiteflies
   - No curative chemical — prevention only

=== FUNGAL DISEASES ===

Powdery Mildew (wide host range)
- Cause: Erysiphe spp.
- Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves and stems
- Control: Sulfur 80 WP @ 3 g/L; Hexaconazole 5 SC @ 1 ml/L

Early Blight of Tomato/Potato
- Cause: Alternaria solani
- Symptoms: Dark brown concentric ring spots on older leaves
- Control: Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2 g/L; Chlorothalonil 75 WP @ 2 g/L

Late Blight (Potato, Tomato)
- Cause: Phytophthora infestans
- Symptoms: Water-soaked patches; white mold on underside; rapid spread
- Control: Cymoxanil + Mancozeb @ 2 g/L; Metalaxyl + Mancozeb @ 2.5 g/L
- Emergency: Spray immediately at first sign; destroy severely affected plants

Fusarium Wilt (Tomato, Cotton, Banana)
- Cause: Fusarium oxysporum (soil-borne)
- Control: Soil drench with Carbendazim 0.1%; Trichoderma viride 5 g/kg soil

=== INTEGRATED PEST MANAGEMENT (IPM) PRINCIPLES ===

1. Prevention: Use certified seeds, resistant varieties, crop rotation
2. Monitoring: Regular field scouting twice/week; pheromone traps
3. Biological control: Conserve natural enemies; use bio-agents
4. Cultural: Deep summer plowing; trap crops; proper spacing
5. Chemical: Use as last resort at threshold levels; prefer selective insecticides
6. Organic: NSKE 5%, neem oil, Bt, NPV, Beauveria bassiana

SAFETY GUIDELINES FOR PESTICIDE USE:
- Wear PPE: gloves, mask, goggles, apron
- Never spray in wind > 15 km/h
- Observe Pre-Harvest Interval (PHI)
- Do not mix more than 2 products
- Store pesticides in original containers away from food and children
- Dispose empty containers safely
